## STEP 1 — Search for Conflicts on USPTO {#step-1-search}

**You must do this before filing. If a similar mark already exists in your class, your application will be rejected.**

### 1.1 Go to the Search Tool

1. Open your browser
2. Go to: **https://tmsearch.uspto.gov/search/search-information**
3. You will see a page titled "Trademark Electronic Search System (TESS)"

### 1.2 Run Your First Search — "LYNKAPP"

1. On the TESS page, click **"Basic Word Mark Search (New User)"**
2. In the "Search Term" box, type: `LYNKAPP`
3. Click the **"Submit Query"** button
4. Review all results
5. Look for any marks in **Classes 9, 38, 42, 35, or 45** that are similar to yours
6. If you see none — ✅ you're likely clear
7. If you see something similar — ⚠️ consult a trademark attorney before proceeding

### 1.3 Run Additional Searches

Repeat the search process for each of these:

| Search Term | Why Search It |
|-------------|---------------|
| `LYNK APP` | Spelling variation |
| `LYNK` | Short form of your name |
| `LINK APP` | Sounds the same (phonetic) |
| `LINKAPP` | Phonetically identical |

### 1.4 Check Search Results

For each result, look at:
- **Status:** Should say "DEAD" (cancelled) to be safe — avoid "LIVE" marks
- **Class(es):** Only worry about Classes 9, 38, 42, 35, 45
- **Goods/Services Description:** Does it cover social media, apps, or messaging?

**If no live, similar marks exist in your relevant classes → proceed to Step 2.**

## STEP 3 — Start the TEAS Application {#step-3-teas}

### 3.1 Navigate to TEAS

1. After logging in, go to: **https://teas.uspto.gov**
2. You will see the "Trademark Electronic Application System (TEAS)" homepage
3. You will see two options:

   **TEAS Plus — $250 per class**
   - Stricter requirements upfront
   - Must use pre-approved goods/services descriptions from the ID Manual
   - Cannot make amendments later without paying extra
   - ✅ **Choose this if you want the cheapest option**

   **TEAS Standard — $350 per class**
   - More flexible descriptions
   - Can write your own goods/services description
   - ✅ **Choose this if you want flexibility**

4. **For LynkApp, we recommend TEAS Standard** because app descriptions are complex
5. Click **"TEAS Standard"**

### 3.2 Select Your Application Type

1. On the next screen, you will see options for the application basis:
   - **Section 1(a) — Already using the mark in commerce** (you have a live app users can access)
   - **Section 1(b) — Intent to Use** (you plan to use it but haven't launched publicly yet)
2. If LynkApp is already live and users can access it → choose **Section 1(a)**
3. If not yet publicly launched → choose **Section 1(b)**
4. Click the appropriate option

### SECTION 4 — Basis for Filing

This section proves you have the right to file.

**If Section 1(a) — Already In Use:**

| Field | What to Enter |
|-------|--------------|
| Date of First Use Anywhere | The earliest date you used the name "LynkApp" (even in development, for internal use) — format: MM/DD/YYYY |
| Date of First Use in Commerce | The date your app was first publicly available across state lines (accessible anywhere in the US or internationally) — format: MM/DD/YYYY |
| Specimen | Upload a screenshot showing "LynkApp" in actual use (see below) |

**What to Upload as Your Specimen:**

The USPTO needs proof the mark is actually being used. For a software app, good specimens are:

- Screenshot of the LynkApp **App Store listing** (showing the name and app icon)
- Screenshot of the **Google Play Store listing** for LynkApp
- Screenshot of the **lynkapp.com website** showing the brand name prominently
- Screenshot of the **app's splash screen** or login screen with "LynkApp" visible
- Screenshot of the **app running** on a phone with "LynkApp" in the header

**Rules for the specimen:**
- Must be a JPG, GIF, PNG, PDF, or MP4
- Must be under 5MB
- Must clearly show the mark "LYNKAPP" as it's being used in commerce
- Cannot be just a mockup or design — must show actual use

**If Section 1(b) — Intent to Use:**
- You do NOT need a specimen yet
- You will need to submit a "Statement of Use" (SOU) within 6 months of filing (extendable up to 3 years)
- Each 6-month extension costs $125 per class
- When your app launches, you'll upload the specimen then

## STEP 6 — After You Submit — What Happens Next {#step-6-after}

### Timeline Overview

```
Day 1:        You submit application and receive Serial Number
Week 1-2:     USPTO logs your application in their system
Month 3-4:    USPTO Examining Attorney reviews your application
Month 4-5:    One of three things happens:
              A) APPROVED for publication → go to next step
              B) OFFICE ACTION sent → you must respond within 3 months
              C) SUSPENDED → wait for related applications to resolve
Month 5-6:    Your mark is PUBLISHED in the Official Gazette (30-day opposition period)
Month 7-8:    If no opposition → you receive Registration Certificate
Total:        8 to 14 months from filing to registration
```

### What is an Office Action?

An Office Action is a letter from the USPTO examining attorney asking you to:
- Clarify or fix your goods/services description
- Submit a different specimen
- Explain why your mark isn't too similar to another mark
- Disclaim part of the mark (e.g., "APP" in LYNKAPP)

**If you receive an Office Action:**
1. You have **3 months** to respond (extendable to 6 months for $125 fee)
2. Log back into your USPTO account at: **https://tsdr.uspto.gov**
3. Enter your serial number to see the Office Action
4. Respond through the TEAS Response to Office Action form
5. If you're unsure how to respond → hire a trademark attorney at this point

### How to Check Your Application Status

1. Go to: **https://tsdr.uspto.gov**
2. In the search box, enter your **Serial Number** (e.g., `98/123,456`)
3. Click **"Status"**
4. You will see the current status of your application
5. Check this every 2-3 weeks

**Application Status Codes:**
| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| PENDING | Application received, waiting for review |
| NON-FINAL ACTION ISSUED | USPTO sent you an Office Action — respond! |
| PUBLISHED FOR OPPOSITION | 30-day window — others can oppose |
| REGISTERED | ✅ Your trademark is approved! Use ® now |
| ABANDONED | Application failed — you need to refile |
| SUSPENDED | Waiting on related application to resolve |

### After Registration

1. You will receive a **Registration Certificate** by mail (physical copy)
2. Your registration number will look like: `Reg. No. 7,123,456`
3. **Switch from ™ to ®** everywhere:
   - App store listing: `LynkApp®`
   - Website: `© 2026 LynkApp®. All rights reserved.`
   - In-app branding: `LynkApp®`

## USING ™ vs ® SYMBOL {#symbols}

| Symbol | When to Use | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **™** | ANY TIME — even before filing, for any unregistered mark you claim | LynkApp™ |
| **℠** | For service marks specifically (optional, rarely used) | LynkApp℠ |
| **®** | ONLY after USPTO officially registers the mark | LynkApp® |

> **Warning:** Using ® before your mark is officially registered is a federal offense. Only use ™ until you receive your registration certificate.

## MAINTENANCE AND RENEWALS {#maintenance}

Once registered, you must file these or your trademark will be cancelled:

### 5-6 Year Mark: Section 8 Declaration of Continued Use

**When:** Between the 5th and 6th anniversary of your registration date

**Where:** https://teas.uspto.gov → "Maintenance/Renewal" → "Section 8"

**What to provide:**
- Specimen showing current use of LYNKAPP (current app screenshot)
- Declaration that the mark is still in use
- **Cost:** $225 per class

**Example:** If registered April 6, 2027 → file between April 6, 2032 and April 6, 2033

### 9-10 Year Mark: Section 8 + Section 9 (Renewal)

**When:** Between the 9th and 10th anniversary of your registration date

**Where:** https://teas.uspto.gov → "Maintenance/Renewal" → "Combined Section 8 & 9"

**What to provide:**
- Same as above, plus renewal filing
- **Cost:** $525 per class

### Every 10 Years After That

- Repeat Section 8 + Section 9 renewal
- **Cost:** $525 per class

### Set Calendar Reminders Now

Create these calendar reminders for after you register:
- **Year 5, Month 1:** "Start preparing Section 8 trademark maintenance"
- **Year 6, Month 11:** "LAST CHANCE — Section 8 deadline is in 1 month"
- **Year 9, Month 1:** "Start preparing Section 8 + 9 trademark renewal"
- **Year 10, Month 11:** "LAST CHANCE — Renewal deadline in 1 month"
